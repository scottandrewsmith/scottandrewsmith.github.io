Bio

I was born and raised in Baltimore, Maryland and had the pleasure of studying mathematics as both an undergrad and grad student at University of Maryland, College Park. I completed my Phd in 2016 advised by Konstantina Trivisa and Sandra Cerrai.

I was a postdoc in Leipzig, Germany from 2016-2019 at the Max Planck Institute for Mathematics in the Sciences in the research groups of Felix Otto and Benjamin Gess. I moved back to the US as a Van Vleck Visiting Assistant Professor from 2019-2021 at University of Wisconsin-Madison in the probability group, collaborating with Hao Shen.

Current position: Since Fall 2021, I'm an associate professor (tenure-track) at the Academy of Mathematics and Systems Sciences within the Chinese Academy of Sciences in Beijing, China.

Research

I enjoy solving challenging technical problems and diving into different aspects of stochastic analysis. I've worked in a number of quite different lines of research within the field of stochastic partial differential equations (SPDE) and benefitted from collaborators spread over three continents. As far as boxes are concerned, I fall somewhere between probability and analysis.
